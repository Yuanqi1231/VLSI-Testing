# Online Multimodal-Telemetry Fusion for Die-to-Die Link Health: A State-of-the-Art Survey and Research Roadmap

## Abstract

Heterogeneous integration has made the die-to-die (D2D) interconnect the new reliability bottleneck of chiplet systems: tens of thousands of micro-bumps per package, sub-10-µm pitches, and in-field degradation modes that no one-time structural test can foresee. The difficulty is not only scale but *confounding*: a single modality cannot tell a benign environmental excursion from an incipient defect, because the two produce overlapping signatures. Deric and Holcomb quantify this directly, measuring an interposer propagation-delay temperature sensitivity of 0.645–1.384 ps/°C and a 24.6-ps (3.70%) drift over a 24.57 °C rise — environmental drift that *exceeds* many anomaly signatures and must be modeled out. This report surveys the state of the art relevant to *online* (runtime, in-field) evaluation of D2D link health by *fusing multimodal on-chip telemetry* — voltage droop/noise, temperature/thermal drift, and PVT sensors — with link-level signals such as bit-error rate (BER), eye margin, lane skew, and retrain/parity events, into a machine-learning (ML) or statistical anomaly/degradation detector. We synthesize nineteen papers read in full, organized into four method families: (i) standards and deterministic structural built-in self-test (BIST), which are rigorous but offline and hard-fault-centric; (ii) on-chip lightweight anomaly engines for telemetry; (iii) ML on signal-integrity / eye representations and general anomaly-detection methods; and (iv) fault diagnosis from electrical/physical signatures. We tabulate evaluation methodologies and expose a field-wide pattern of simulation/proprietary data, injected anomalies, divergent metrics, and the absence of any shared public D2D benchmark. Within the surveyed corpus and our targeted gap searches, we find a precise empty intersection — no work fuses voltage, thermal, and link telemetry for online D2D link health — and propose a concrete, course-aligned, DATE-2027-Track-T research program: an autoencoder/LSTM multimodal-fusion detector benchmarked against deterministic BIST, on-chip unsupervised detectors, and non-ML statistical fusion, evaluated on a released, physically grounded, *characterized* fault-injection benchmark whose realism is validated against measured adjacent-channel data. We are explicit that the contribution is a *domain + benchmark* contribution rather than a new ML method, and that every numeric claim about D2D telemetry currently rests on simulation, standards, or adjacent-domain measurement.

## 1. Introduction and Motivation

### 1.1 The chiplet shift and why the interconnect is the bottleneck

The economics of advanced silicon have driven a structural change in how large systems are built. Rather than fabricating one monolithic die at the largest reticle, vendors now *disaggregate* a system into multiple smaller chiplets — often on different process nodes and from different fabs — and reassemble them on a silicon interposer, an embedded bridge, or a fan-out redistribution layer. Chakravarty's Intel position paper makes the scale concrete: Ponte Vecchio integrates 47 chiplets across five process nodes and more than 100 billion transistors, and the number of die-to-die interconnects in a single package has climbed from under 1,000 (Lakefield) to just under 100,000 (Ponte Vecchio), with I/O density rising from over 400 IOs/mm² today toward a projected 10,000 IOs/mm² and interconnect pitch dropping below 10 µm [N6]. (The same Ponte Vecchio interconnect count is reported variously as ~75,000–100,000 across sources; we use each source's own figure and note the spread where both appear.) Chuang et al. corroborate the *direction* of these trends with measured imec data — TSV/micro-bump RC parasitics and SEM defect images — and with standardized industry bump maps drawn from real JEDEC HBM2 (1,968 interconnects) and UCIe Advanced Package (225 interconnects) structures [N3]; the headline density and pitch roadmap figures (>400 → 10,000 IOs/mm², 400 → 36 → projected 10 µm pitch) are Chakravarty's [N6], which N3 echoes as a trend rather than measures.

This explosion in interconnect count is precisely what makes the D2D link the reliability bottleneck. The classic 3D-IC test survey by Lee and Chakrabarty established the governing premise more than a decade ago: a single TSV/interconnect defect between two layers can void the entire die stack, and a random-bonding strategy of untested dies decreases overall yield *exponentially* with layer count [N7]. With each interconnect now carrying signal, power, or clock through a micro-bump, an RDL wire, and possibly a TSV, the package has thousands of independent failure opportunities, several of which manifest only after deployment — through electromigration, thermal-mechanical fatigue at C4 bumps, NBTI/PBTI-driven drift, and warpage-induced clustered defects [N5, N7].

### 1.2 Why online health monitoring, not one-time structural test

The dominant industrial answer to interconnect defects is structural test: load deterministic patterns through IEEE 1838 Die Wrapper Registers, capture pass/fail, diagnose, and repair using spare lanes. This is rigorous and provably correct for the defects it targets, but it is fundamentally a *manufacturing-time, one-shot* activity. It cannot observe the *weak, parametric, drift, and aging* failure modes that develop in the field. Chuang et al. emphasize that resistive ("weak") shorts and opens manifest as *timing degradation* — slow-to-rise/slow-to-fall behavior — rather than as logic errors [N3], the kind of slow-onset change that only a runtime monitor watching BER, eye margin, and lane skew over time can catch. Chakravarty explicitly calls for "in-field test and repair" of *aging-related failures* to "increase availability" as one of six DfT features the community must standardize [N6].

We are candid about the limits of this motivation. The argument for the *online* axis specifically — as opposed to better manufacturing test or burn-in screening — rests on the *existence* of slow-onset parametric/aging modes (documented degradation physics in [N5, N7] and Chakravarty's standards call in [N6]) rather than on a published field-failure rate for D2D parametric drift. No surveyed paper reports a D2D in-field FIT rate or RMA statistic; the physics that those modes exist and are invisible to logic test is established, but their *prevalence* relative to manufacturing-screened defects is, in the open literature, an order-of-magnitude argument rather than a measured one. We treat this as a stated limitation rather than an established quantity. The thesis of this report is that the in-field axis is best addressed not by replaying structural tests but by *continuously evaluating link health from the telemetry the chip already exposes*.

### 1.3 Why multimodal telemetry and AI

A D2D link does not degrade in isolation. Its eye margin and BER are modulated by supply-voltage droop and noise, by temperature and thermal gradients, and by process variation captured in PVT sensors. The in-development IEEE Std P3405 and the UCIe standard formalize exactly this telemetry — Rx eye margining, BER measurement, lane-to-lane skew, lane retrain/repair status, and on-die PVT/process-health-indicator (PHI) sensors [#6] — so the raw modalities are *standard-mandated*, not hypothetical, on every compliant link. Yet a *single* modality is confounded: a temperature rise and a genuine link defect can both shrink the eye. Deric and Holcomb quantify this confounding directly, measuring a non-uniform 0.645–1.384 ps/°C temperature sensitivity of interposer propagation delay and a 24.6-ps (3.70%) drift over a 24.57 °C rise — drift that *exceeds* many anomaly signatures and must be separated out [N4]. Distinguishing a benign environmental excursion from an incipient link failure therefore requires *fusing* the modalities so that the environmental common mode can be modeled and removed. This is an inherently statistical / ML problem: labeled field failures are scarce, the normal operating manifold is high-dimensional and PVT-dependent, and degradation is gradual. Anomaly-detection methods that learn the normal manifold from abundant healthy data and flag deviations — autoencoders, density scoring, distance-to-center — are the natural fit, and are exactly the methods the course recommends (Autoencoder, LSTM, statistical anomaly detection) for this direction.

### 1.4 Contributions, scope, and search method

This report makes four contributions. First, it consolidates the *verified* facts of nineteen papers — eleven Tier-1 works read against their PDFs plus eight added papers (N1–N8) with ground-truth extractions — into a coherent state of the art for D2D link health. Second, it organizes that state of the art into four method families and, for each, distills what is reusable for an online multimodal detector. Third, it analyzes the field's evaluation practices and articulates the bar a DATE Track-T paper must clear. Fourth, it identifies the precise research gap and proposes a concrete, course-rubric-aligned project to fill it.

**Search method and its limits.** The corpus was assembled by bibliography-mining from an initial verified Tier-1 seed set, followed by targeted gap searches along three axes the thesis demands: multimodal fusion for reliability, online resource-constrained on-chip detectors, and the physical grounding of the D2D signal set plus the structural baselines an ML detector must beat. Inclusion required a peer-reviewed or preprint full text with extractable methods and results; vendor white-papers and un-obtained items were excluded (see References note). This is a *seeded, targeted* sample, not a systematic survey — so every negative claim below ("no work fuses…") is scoped to *the surveyed set plus our targeted searches*, not asserted as a proof of field-wide absence.

**Scope and honesty about domain adjacency.** A central caveat threads through everything below: *none* of the nineteen papers is a native, online, multimodal ML anomaly detector for a standardized UCIe/BoW/AIB D2D link. The works cluster around the target without occupying it. Some are D2D-native but deterministic and hard-fault-centric (#3, #4, #6, N3, N5, N6, N7, N8); some carry the right ML methods but on adjacent channels — DRAM I/O (#1, N1), SATA HSIO (#7), TSV S-parameters (#12), Cu-interconnect reflection (#13), generic benchmarks (N2-D3); some are on-die anomaly engines but for CPU telemetry (#2), NoC thermal (#11), or interposer PDN/PUF (#14, N4). We flag this adjacency throughout, and every entry's metadata was verified against its PDF or a ground-truth extraction.

## 2. Background

### 2.1 The D2D interconnect, the standards landscape, and the standard-mandated signal set

A modern D2D interface is a wide, parallel, source-synchronous PHY operating over very short reach. UCIe, AIB (Advanced Interface Bus), and BoW (Bunch of Wires) all share this character: many single-ended or differential lanes, micro-bumps connecting two dies through an interposer or bridge, RDL routing, and — critically for this report — controllable receive-clock delay lines for source-synchronous timing [N4]. A complete UCIe interface comprises roughly 225–240 micro-bumps arranged in hexagonal or rectangular arrays, and the standard provisions *spare interconnects* plus repair MUXes so that a defective lane can be remapped [N8].

The standards landscape that governs test and repair of these links is layered, and it is where the telemetry this report fuses is formally defined. IEEE Std 1838-2019 defines the Die Wrapper Register (DWR) and the test-access architecture for 3D stacks; IEEE 1500 core wrappers and IEEE 1149.1 JTAG provide module- and chip-level access [N7, N8]. Chakravarty identifies the explicit *gap* in this stack: IEEE 1838 gives die-level (SCM) and SoC-level (FPP) access but does *not* address testing of the functional interfaces *between* dice [N6]. Marinissen et al.'s IEEE Std P3405 is the in-development standard aimed squarely at this gap [#6]. It is the most on-target standards artifact in the corpus and serves as the *schema spine* for the whole thesis: beyond formalizing the Interconnect-Repair Problem over repair chains with spare interconnects and proposing a machine-readable repair-description language, P3405 enumerates the exact on-die telemetry the detector consumes — eye width/height, jitter, signal swing, lane-to-lane skew, lane-repair status, and PVT/PHI sensors [#6]. UCIe complements this by standardizing the micro-bump map, the spare/repair scheme, and the on-die margining/BER telemetry; Wang et al. analyze and *improve* the UCIe repair map to eliminate non-repairable power-ground shorts [N8]. The point worth carrying into Sections 5 and 6 is that the modalities the detector fuses are not a wish-list — P3405 and UCIe *require* them.

### 2.2 A failure/anomaly taxonomy

The literature supports a clean two-axis taxonomy of D2D interconnect failures.

**Hard faults** are the classical structural defects: stuck-at (open → SA0/SA1), bridge/short between adjacent interconnects (modeled as wired-AND/wired-OR), and shorts to power/ground. Bhoumik et al. map Cu-pillar and RDL physical defects (cracks, opens, misalignment, bridging) to equivalent RLC circuits and derive defect-size thresholds for when a short becomes a stuck-at fault (e.g., a signal short to VDD with R < 500 Ω → SA1; a critical radial short path of ~3 nm for Cu bumps) [#3]. Huang et al.'s ARCS targets the same micro-bump shorts/opens → stuck-at + wired-OR/AND classes [#4]. Wang et al. ground the UCIe fault model: hard short = wired-AND/OR, open = stuck-at, with Intel-reported statistics that shorts dominate (~1/3 data-to-power/ground, ~1/3 two-data, ~1/3 more-than-two-data) and opens are ~5% of defects [N8].

**Weak / parametric / drift / aging faults** are the subtler, in-field-relevant class. Chuang et al. introduce technology-independent models for them: a *weak open* is a single-net slow-to-rise/slow-to-fall (timing) fault; a *weak short* is a multi-net slow-to-rise/fall; and *coupling* defects (capacitive/inductive crosstalk between adjacent fine-pitch lanes) appear as victim-aggressor timing faults or, in the worst case, a Maximal-Aggressor fault [N3]. Cui et al. emphasize *physics-related* defects that generic patterns miss — coupling between neighboring fine-pitch interconnects and *clustered* faults caused by wafer-thinning-induced warpage and uneven stress around TSVs/micro-bumps [N5]. Lee and Chakrabarty catalog the degradation mechanisms behind these: delamination, peeling, cracking, voids, dislocations, edge effects, and thermal fatigue at C4 bumps [N7]. This weak/parametric/drift class — invisible to logic test, manifesting as gradual timing/margin loss — is precisely what an online telemetry-fusion detector targets.

### 2.3 The observable telemetry modalities and which paper exposes each

The thesis fuses three broad modality groups; the literature exposes each, though — as the honesty note below records — with very uneven directness.

| Modality | What it captures | Anchoring papers |
|---|---|---|
| **Link eye / BER / skew / retrain** | Rx eye width/height, bit-error rate, lane-to-lane skew, retrain/parity/repair events | P3405-mandated set [#6] (eye width/height, jitter, swing, lane skew, lane-repair status); #7 (163 eye-width/height + Rx calibration codes); #8 (eye-diagram images of crosstalk/loss/reflection); N6 (inter-lane skew measurement, clocking); N8 (lane remap/repair events) |
| **Voltage droop / noise** | Supply droop, di/dt, ground bounce, power-noise | #2 (Plundervolt voltage-droop *injection* — an attack stimulus, not a telemetry stream); #3/N3/N5 (ground-bounce / SSOL constraints during switching, as a switching-noise proxy); N4 (RO-based supply heating/droop stress); N7 (IR-drop and di/dt in 3D) |
| **Temperature / thermal drift** | Per-region/per-router temperature, thermal gradients | #11 (per-router thermal features); N4 (quantified 1.002 ps/°C avg delay sensitivity to temperature); N7 (thermal effects "greatly exacerbated" in 3D, upper tiers more vulnerable) |
| **PVT sensors** | Process/voltage/temperature monitors, process-health indicators | #6 (PVT/PHI sensors mandated as on-die telemetry); #2 (sensor-domain telemetry, but CPU MSRs, not D2D PVT) |
| **Impedance / reflection signatures** | S-parameters, reflection coefficient, PDN impedance, propagation delay | #12 (TSV S11/S21/phase, HFSS); #13 (full S11 pattern, measured Cu); #14 (on-die PDN impedance sweep); N4 (interposer propagation delay) |

*Honesty note on modality sourcing.* Every modality is cited, but coverage is uneven, and this unevenness is itself part of the gap. The eye/BER/skew, thermal, and impedance rows are well-sourced. The **voltage-droop** row is the weakest: no surveyed *detector* consumes a dedicated runtime supply-droop sensor stream — #2's Plundervolt is an attack injection, and #3/N3/N5 expose voltage only indirectly as switching/ground-bounce constraints. The **PVT** row is effectively single-sourced: only P3405 (#6) genuinely standardizes PVT/PHI as a consumable on-die stream; #2's "sensor domain" is CPU model-specific registers, not D2D PVT. Thus, while the *standards* mandate voltage-droop and PVT telemetry, no surveyed detector *ingests* a dedicated droop or D2D-PVT stream — which is exactly part of the white space the proposed work targets. The broader point stands: *no single paper exposes more than one or two of these modalities together*, and none fuses across the three groups for online D2D health.

Having established the modalities the detector must fuse, we now examine how each method family handles them.

## 3. State of the Art by Method Family

### 3.1 Standards and deterministic structural BIST/DfT — the rigorous-but-offline baseline

This family is the most D2D-native and the most rigorous; it defines both the baseline an online detector must complement and the *defect physics* that make synthetic data realistic. It is also the most internally redundant family — a single *constant-pattern I-ATPG lineage* runs through several papers — so we treat that lineage compactly and reserve detail for what is reusable.

**Problem framing.** Detect (and ideally diagnose and repair) manufacturing shorts/opens in the micro-bump/TSV/RDL array, deterministically, at manufacturing test, with provable coverage and minimal pattern count.

**The constant-pattern I-ATPG lineage (#3, N3, N5, N8).** A coherent body of work formulates the bump-short problem as graph coloring over placement-aware adjacency neighborhoods and achieves near-constant pattern counts independent of interconnect count. Bhoumik et al. (#3) model the hexagonal bump-short problem (4 codewords for 12 neighbors) and detect all stuck-at and bridging faults with exactly *three* patterns {011, 110, 101} via wired-AND/wired-OR detectors, proving 95.61% diagnosability, backed by a Q3D→HSPICE defect-size-to-fault-threshold flow on ASAP7. Chuang et al.'s E2I-TEST (N3) is the most thorough public treatment: placement-aware neighborhoods (8 aggressors rectangular, 12 hexagonal), technology-independent weak-defect and coupling fault models, a graph-coloring formulation needing only K=4 colors, and a *constant* 16-pattern count independent of interconnect count k — with ground-bounce mitigation via a Simultaneous-Switching-Outputs-Limit (SSOL=1) constraint and a diagnosis extension. Wang et al. (N8) port the same 16-pattern, aliasing-free, hard+weak-defect I-ATPG to UCIe and additionally redesign the UCIe bump map to eliminate non-repairable catastrophic power-ground shorts (Alt maps → 100% single-short repairable, 0 catastrophic). Cui et al. (N5) add a *physical-aware* BIST emitting layout-derived deterministic patterns (one-hot, checkerboard, stripe, solid) to activate coupling and clustered faults, plus a multi-level shifting repair sharing redundant interconnects across groups and an *ATE-free*, fully on-die BISR that auto-programs eFUSE. Huang et al.'s ARCS (#4) is the logarithmic-pattern variant: exploiting the logical h×w micro-bump array gives a pattern count of 2·(⌈log₂h⌉+⌈log₂w⌉) with homogeneity compression and a split TX/RX wrapper (RX uses XNOR-vs-golden + OR-tree).

**Standards/position/foundational papers.** Marinissen et al.'s P3405 (#6) supplies the formal vocabulary (Interconnect-Repair Problem, repair chains, spare lanes, Tx/Rx repair MUXes), the feature/fault-mode list, *and* the standard-mandated telemetry schema this report builds on (§2.1). Chakravarty (N6) is the call-to-standardize that names the IEEE 1838 inter-die gap and the in-field test/repair and inter-lane-skew features. Lee and Chakrabarty (N7) is the foundational 3D-IC test survey establishing prebond testability, known-good-die (KGD), thermal/power stressors, and — relevant as a structural baseline the detector complements — TSV self-repair.

**Data, results, reusability.** Data here is simulation + measured parasitics + public standardized maps: #3 uses Q3D/HSPICE on ASAP7; N3 validates RC-sim Shmoo detectability (a two-cycle at-speed opposing-pattern test detects open/short/coupling 4/3/2 orders of magnitude weaker than a one-cycle test) and reports pattern count/coverage on Intel Ponte Vecchio (~75,000 interconnects per N3), HBM2 (1,968), and UCIe (225); #4 reports 99.9% detection-time and 53.5% area reduction vs IEEE 1500; N5 shows 30–100 test cycles regardless of array size and ~1–2 orders-of-magnitude lower repair cost than router/ring/R2 baselines. **What is reusable for the thesis:** (1) the defect taxonomy and defect→fault thresholds (#3 Table III, N3's weak/coupling models) are *free ground-truth labels* for a synthetic D2D fault corpus; (2) the realistic per-lane topology and adjacency neighborhoods (N3's HBM2/UCIe maps, 8/12 aggressors) give physically grounded link structure; (3) the RX XNOR-vs-golden + OR-tree and the lane-repair/remap event are themselves *link-level signals* the detector can fuse; (4) the ground-bounce/SSOL discussion connects switching activity to the voltage-noise modality. Crucially, none of these are ML, none are online, and all explicitly leave weak/parametric/drift/aging *in-field* monitoring out of scope — which is exactly the white space.

### 3.2 On-chip lightweight anomaly engines — the deployment story

This family answers the load-bearing question for an *online* thesis: how would a detector actually run on-die, cheaply, in the field, and respond? Because it is the deployment heart of the proposal, we draw out the operational requirements it establishes.

**OCTANE (#2)** is the strongest peer template. Ortega et al. build an unsupervised, HW/SW-co-designed detector over telemetry feature vectors using two fixed-point distance scores — a variance-norm Euclidean d_E and a cosine-derived d_C — computed *per domain* (core/memory/sensor) and aggregated by mean or product, implemented with Kogge-Stone adders and Wallace-tree multipliers. Feature ranking is workload-independent via a normalized-mutual-information index. It is evaluated on *measured* telemetry from two real Intel CPUs (181/460 features) with injected Spectre/Rowhammer/Plundervolt anomalies, reaching AUROC > 0.95 in most cases, with a cloud-side XGBoost diagnosis stage at accuracy/F1 0.96/0.98 (peak 0.999 when scores are combined), at 1.2% die-area and ~0.5% average power overhead (2.6% at idle), beating its own prior work E-SCOUT. The operational template it establishes is precisely the one the thesis needs: (a) *fixed-point distance scoring* cheap enough for on-die; (b) a *hierarchical per-domain → aggregated* score that maps directly onto per-lane → per-link; (c) *MI-based robust feature selection*; (d) a *two-stage edge-detect / cloud-diagnose split* (ML flags and ranks at the edge; a heavier stage diagnoses); and (e) the *AUROC + PPA* reporting rubric. Its non-multimodal, non-D2D, CPU-MSR scope is the novelty wedge — it shows the machinery is feasible at ~1% area but never fuses link telemetry.

**HeatSense (#11)** is the deliberately *non-ML* counterweight and supplies the response-and-cost half of the deployment story. Hasanzadeh et al. detect thermal-sensor spoofing in NoC routers with multi-tiered sigma thresholds (Mean ± K·σ) where σ is approximated by a *bit-shift* (σₙ = Mean/2ⁿ) and the running mean by an approximated weighted moving average, yielding a 3-level *graded* anomaly classification feeding a *proportional, randomized port-shutdown countermeasure with staged recovery*. In simulation it reaches up to 82% accuracy — 10–15% below Random Forest — while saving up to 75% logic and eliminating DSP/BUFGMUX. Three things transfer directly: the *multiplier-free ASIC-friendly statistical template* (a realistic floor when an LSTM will not fit on-die); the *graded multi-tier response* (→ graded lane throttle/spare-lane remap rather than a binary trip); and an explicitly *honest accuracy-vs-area trade-off*. Its *trust-separation threat model* — untrusted analog/PHY IP, trusted detector — maps cleanly onto multi-vendor chiplet integration. It is a NoC-thermal, not a D2D-link, paper.

**Deployment requirements this family establishes.** Together, #2 and #11 fix the bar an online D2D detector must clear: a roughly ~1%-area / sub-1%-average-power on-die budget; hierarchical per-element → per-aggregate scoring; a graded (not binary) response coupled to the existing repair/remap mechanism; MI-based feature selection; a two-stage flag-then-diagnose architecture; and explicit PPA reporting against a structural baseline. The physical-sensing works of §3.4 (#14, N4) extend this with the demonstration that the interposer fabric itself can be sensed fully on-die with negligible footprint. No member of this family fuses modalities or consumes D2D link telemetry — that absence is the deployment-side statement of the gap.

### 3.3 ML on signal-integrity / eye representations and anomaly-detection methods

This family carries the right *methods* for the thesis — but almost entirely on adjacent channels, and (as §6 owns explicitly) none of these methods is itself novel; the contribution will be their combination and domain, not the machinery.

**Usama et al. (#1)** is the closest method anchor. They jointly train an autoencoder (R¹⁰⁰→R¹¹→R¹⁰⁰) and a single-layer classifier on a simulated 10 Gbps mobile-DRAM channel with the loss L = ‖x − x̂‖² − y·log(ŷ), where the classifier gradient flows *only from valid (normal) data* — a small, principled tweak that lifts accuracy from 76.3% (both-gradients) to 93.1%. The frozen encoder feeds LOF/LSAnomaly/NN detectors (NN 95.9%), and a Fermat-Weber (geometric-median) latent anchor enables signal-integrity enhancement (11.3% average eye-area gain). The *valid-only-gradient* recipe needs only abundant normal data — the D2D data reality — and is course-friendly. It is DRAM I/O (not D2D), all-simulated, with no on-die cost; it also releases code, useful as an adjacent-channel validation set.

**Medico et al. (N1)** is the originating unsupervised SI-AD method #1 builds on. They combine a Contractive Autoencoder (reconstruction loss + Jacobian-Frobenius penalty for robust features) with the density-based Local Outlier Factor on sliding windows of a digital-counter waveform under clock jitter, training *only on anomaly-free data*, and localize the jitter-violating regions with no false alarms (qualitative; ADS-simulated, single case study, no quantitative metrics). The *train-on-normal → contractive-latent → density-score* recipe applied per sliding window directly models a SI degradation mechanism (jitter → timing violation) that is a proxy for D2D eye/skew loss. It is single-modality and offline.

**Miao et al. (#8)** treat the eye as an *image*: a CNN classifies 7 SI-impairment classes (crosstalk/loss/reflection + composites) from ADS-simulated eye diagrams, localizes the impaired line, and regresses a fixing parameter, reaching 100% accuracy at 10 epochs — a clear small-set overfitting caveat (the paper itself notes ≥80% of the data is needed for stability). The *simulator-sweep-then-label* pipeline and the *eye-as-image impairment-type classification* are the transferable ideas.

**Sanchez-Martinez et al. (#7)** frame post-silicon SATA-link risk as supervised pass/fail classification over 163 eye/calibration features from measured Intel silicon (1,275 samples), with Boosted Trees best at 94.84% and the full feature set beating PCA-7 (p < 1e-8). The transferable parts are the *eye-width/height + Rx-calibration feature set* as D2D margining telemetry, the *SVM/NN/BT + accuracy/sensitivity/specificity* harness, and the lesson that richer raw PHY features beat aggressive PCA. It is a batch qualification decision, not online, and supervised, not novelty detection.

**Ruff et al. — Deep SAD (N2-D3)** is the canonical, domain-agnostic semi-supervised method to instantiate the detector. It generalizes unsupervised Deep SVDD to exploit a large *unlabeled* (mostly-normal, possibly polluted) set plus a *small* pool of labeled normals *and* labeled anomalies, by pulling normals toward a fixed hypersphere center c and pushing anomalies away via an inverse-squared-distance loss — imposing *no cluster assumption on the anomaly class*. The anomaly score s(x)=‖φ(x)−c‖ is a single forward pass; AE pre-training initializes the encoder. On CIFAR-10 it rises from 60.9 (no labels) to 81.9 AUC (20% labeled anomalies), and critically *generalizes to unseen anomaly classes* at test time where a supervised classifier collapses *under scarce labels and on complex data (CIFAR-10)* — on simpler MNIST/F-MNIST the supervised baseline catches up as the number of known anomaly classes grows. **Why it fits the thesis:** this is *exactly* the runtime regime — bulk telemetry unlabeled and mostly healthy, a few verified-healthy and a few known-degradation episodes labeled; heterogeneous link failure modes (thermal vs droop vs eye collapse vs skew) demand the no-cluster-assumption + generalization-to-novel-modes property; and the pollution-robustness scenario models field telemetry secretly containing undetected degradation. It uses only public image/tabular benchmarks and no time-series — the mapping to D2D telemetry is inferred, not demonstrated.

### 3.4 Fault diagnosis and integrity from electrical/physical signatures

This family shows that interconnect health can be read from *electrical/physical signatures* when imaging or full logic test is infeasible — a strong feature source, and the source of the confounder-handling recipe the fusion thesis most needs.

**Huang et al. (#12)** classify TSV void/short/open from HFSS-simulated two-port S-parameters (S11/S21/phase, 200 MHz–20 GHz), with Random Forest substantially beating KNN and the *low-frequency band (≤5 GHz) most separable* (the TSV's MOS-capacitor behavior); it can even regress void amount (0–35%). **Kang et al. — SREL (#13)** learn the *full frequency-domain S11 pattern* (measured Cu interconnects, VNA 0–14 GHz, 790 samples) and decompose root-cause + ordinal-severity into a bank of binary classifiers, reaching 99.3% accuracy / 0.991 macro-F1 (vs 98.6% softmax) with the best noise robustness under a 5/10/15 dB Gaussian-noise sweep. **Monfared et al. — ChipletQuake (#14)** sense the *shared interposer PDN impedance* with a fully on-die, fully-digital actuator+TDC mesh on a real FPGA (3 SLR chiplets), detecting interposer SLL-count changes (129→133), dormant Hardware Trojans, and tampering via Welch's t-test (|t|>4.5) + Wasserstein distance against a golden trace — with no signal interface and no extra hardware.

**Deric and Holcomb (N4)** add the security-flavored, *online*, per-wire physical-telemetry anchor, and supply the confounder-handling recipe the fusion thesis most needs. A between-die delay PUF over interposer Super-Long-Lines measures picosecond propagation delay by clock-phase sweeping (14.286 ps/step), validated across dozens of real FPGAs. The differential cell pairs two SLLs so clock/environmental variation cancels as *common mode*; a ridge-regression additive-bias model and a *sensor-free* aggregate-mean linear temperature compensation separate environmental drift from anomaly. It reports mean within-class 68.3 ps vs between-class 847.7 ps (>5× separation), temperature sensitivity 1.002 ps/°C avg (range 0.645–1.384), no measurable aging effect, equal-error FPR=FNR≈2.3e-24, and a footprint of 0.27% LUTs / 0.815 W; it argues a 0.04 pF probe load adds ~4% delay and is thus detectable. **Why it is pivotal for the thesis:** the *differential common-mode rejection*, the *additive-bias regression with residual analysis*, and the *sensor-free aggregate-mean temperature compensation* are directly transferable statistical templates for handling thermal/voltage confounders — *the* central problem of multimodal fusion. It is PUF/authentication framing (within/between-class distance), an FPGA SSI emulator (not a productized UCIe PHY), single-modality (delay), and reports no BER/eye/link metrics.

**What is reusable across 3.4:** electrical signatures (S-parameters, reflection, PDN impedance, propagation delay) as features when you cannot image the link; the low-frequency-band stimulus tip (#12); the golden-vs-measured statistical-distance decision rule (#14, N4); the noise-injection robustness protocol (#13); and — most importantly — the differential/regression confounder-handling (N4).

Six evaluation patterns emerge, and they define the bar a DATE paper must clear:

- **Simulation and proprietary data dominate.** A majority are pure simulation (#1, #3, #8, #11, #12, N1, and the simulation part of N3), and the "measured" works use proprietary silicon (#2, #7) or lab vehicles / FPGA emulators (#13, #14, N4). **No shared public D2D-link anomaly benchmark exists** — the field has no WM-811K equivalent.
- **Anomalies are almost always injected/synthetic.** ISI/amplitude/harmonic (#1), Spectre/Rowhammer/Plundervolt (#2), EDA parameter sweeps (#8), laser-cut cracks / chamber corrosion (#13), graph-coloring fault models (#3, N3, N8), injected RC defects (N3). This *legitimizes* a fault-injection strategy for a course/DATE project — it is the field norm — provided the generator is *characterized and released* rather than asserted realistic (§6.4).
- **Metrics diverge and are not cross-comparable.** Detection uses AUROC (#2), bare accuracy (#1, #7, #8, #12, #13), statistical-distance thresholds (#14, N4), or qualitative visualization (N1); BIST papers report diagnosability/coverage/pattern-count/PPA (#3, #4, N3, N5, N8). Several report *no* headline number (#12, #14, N1). A DATE detector should report AUROC and precision/recall/F1, not bare accuracy, given the inevitable normal ≫ anomalous imbalance.
- **PPA is reported by exactly the on-chip works** (#2, #3, #4, #11, N4, N5) and *ignored by every pure-ML paper* (#1, #7, #8, #12, #13, N1, N2-D3). A detector claiming "on-die" must report PPA against a structural baseline; an offline analyzer need not, but must say so up front.
- **Robustness and reproducibility are thin.** Only #13 and N4 do principled noise/drift sweeps; only #1, #13, N2-D3 give code; suspiciously perfect numbers (#8 100%, #2 combined diagnosis 0.999) on small sets signal overfitting risk. A noise/drift robustness sweep is cheap differentiation.
- **No work fuses modalities and beats a deterministic baseline at the same time.** The structural baselines (#3, #4, N3, N5) detect hard faults cheaply; an ML detector must justify itself on what they cannot do — weak/parametric/drift/aging, online, from telemetry — and must include the strong cheap baselines (RF/Boosted Trees/XGBoost) that repeatedly win in #2/#7/#12 so any deep model's delta is provable.

## 5. Synthesis: What Is Solved vs Open

**What is solved.** Deterministic structural test of D2D micro-bump arrays is mature and rigorous: hard shorts/opens are detected, diagnosed, and repaired with provable coverage and near-constant pattern counts (#3, #4, N3, N5, N8), and the standards scaffolding (IEEE 1838 DWR, IEEE 1500, the in-development P3405, UCIe repair) is in place (#6, N6, N7, N8). The *methods* for learning anomalies under label scarcity are also mature — train-on-normal autoencoders with density scoring (#1, N1), valid-only-gradient discriminative latents (#1), semi-supervised no-cluster-assumption detectors that generalize to unseen anomalies (N2-D3), and electrical-signature classification/severity-rating (#12, #13). On-chip *deployment* is demonstrated: lightweight fixed-point or non-ML detectors with hierarchical per-domain scoring and graded response at ~1% area (#2, #11), and on-die physical sensing of the interposer fabric itself (#14, N4).

**The precise gap.** *No work in the surveyed set, nor in our targeted gap searches, fuses multimodal voltage + thermal + link telemetry for online D2D link-health evaluation.* The analogs split cleanly into three non-overlapping camps:

1. **Multimodal fusion for reliability exists only off-target.** This is the gap's load-bearing claim, so we are precise about its evidentiary basis and its limit. *Within the surveyed corpus*, the only multimodal-fusion-for-reliability work is adjacent: §3.4's electrical-signature fusion (#12, #13, #14, N4) fuses *frequency/impedance* features but for hard-fault diagnosis on TSV/Cu/PDN channels, never consuming link-level BER/eye/skew/retrain, and never online-for-D2D. **We do not claim to have surveyed the broader prognostics-and-health-management (PHM) literature** (SoC/critical-path end-of-life prognostics via PCA/ICA/AE fusion of embedded instruments, GA-degradation models, cross-layer SEU/aging fusion, GPU-cluster telemetry). That literature exists, it fuses voltage/thermal/aging sensors, and a referee who works in it could rightly point to it. The honest, defensible statement is therefore *narrow and twofold*: (a) no surveyed work, and to our knowledge no published work, fuses the *D2D link-level* signal set (BER, eye margin, lane skew, retrain/parity) *together with* voltage and thermal telemetry; and (b) consequently the contribution is a *domain + benchmark re-targeting* of known fusion machinery to D2D, not a new fusion method. A full DATE submission must convert this paragraph into a per-paper table of cited PHM works stating exactly what each fuses and why D2D link telemetry lies outside its input set; absent that, the broad form of the claim is the single largest reject risk and we do not make it here.
2. **D2D-native work is deterministic, hard-fault, structural, and offline** (#3, #4, #6, N3, N5, N6, N7, N8) — explicitly leaving weak/parametric/drift/aging *in-field* monitoring out of scope.
3. **Online on-die detectors are single-modality** and aimed elsewhere — CPU counters (#2), NoC thermal (#11), PDN impedance or delay (#14, N4) — and the security-flavored ones are single-modality by construction.

Nobody in the surveyed set streams eye-margin + BER + lane-skew + retrain/parity + PVT (voltage, temperature) *together* into an online degradation detector, even though P3405 and UCIe mandate that exact signal set. The intersection of multimodal PVT+link fusion (camp 1), online on-die operation (camp 3), and D2D specificity with weak/drift fault coverage (camp 2's omission) is where no surveyed work sits, and is the contribution. We state this once here; later sections refer back to it as "the gap of §5" rather than restating it.

## 6. Research Opportunity: The Proposed Project

### 6.1 Problem statement

*Given the multimodal telemetry a UCIe/P3405-class D2D link already exposes by standard mandate — per-lane eye margin and BER, lane-to-lane skew, retrain/parity/repair events, plus on-chip voltage droop/noise, temperature/thermal drift, and PVT sensors — continuously evaluate link health at runtime and flag gradual degradation (weak/parametric/drift/aging) before it becomes a hard failure, distinguishing genuine link degradation from benign PVT excursions.* This is a concrete, bounded, course-appropriate problem (a small problem, small data, small system) that sits in the white space of §5. The contribution is framed honestly as *domain + a released, characterized benchmark + the specific multimodal-D2D combination + the N4 confounder-rejection recipe* — **not** as a new ML method. We make no novelty claim on the AE/LSTM/Deep-SAD machinery, all of which is well-trodden.

### 6.2 The proposed detector

A two-stage, hierarchical, multimodal-fusion detector:

1. **Per-lane temporal feature embedding.** Stream each lane's telemetry through a sliding window (à la N1) and fuse the modalities into one latent per window. Two interchangeable backbones: (a) an **autoencoder with valid-only-gradient** training à la #1 (train on abundant healthy data; gradient from valid samples keeps the normal manifold tight); (b) an **LSTM temporal-autoencoder** over the per-lane sequence (eye margin, BER, retrain count, skew, voltage, temperature over time). Modeling the time axis is, to our knowledge, not done by any surveyed paper for D2D telemetry — but temporal/LSTM autoencoders for time-series anomaly detection are a standard, well-established technique, so we claim the *application* of temporal modeling to fused D2D telemetry, not the technique. Confounder handling borrows N4's *differential common-mode rejection* and *sensor-free aggregate-mean temperature compensation* plus an additive-bias regression to subtract environmental drift before scoring — this is the mechanism by which fusion is expected to beat single-modality thresholding.
2. **Hierarchical per-lane → per-link graded scoring.** Score each lane (reconstruction error, or distance-to-center s(x)=‖φ(x)−c‖ if Deep SAD (N2-D3) is used to exploit a few labeled degradation episodes), aggregate per-lane scores to a per-link score by mean or product (#2), and emit a graded 3-level anomaly classification feeding a proportional response (graded lane throttle / spare-lane remap recommendation) à la #2/#11. A deterministic BIST/structural check (#3/#4/N3) confirms before any repair — ML flags and ranks; a deterministic check verifies (echoing #2's edge-detect/cloud-diagnose split).

To keep the architecture committed rather than a menu, the eye-as-image front-end (#8) and a contractive latent (N1) are noted as *optional, evaluated-only-if-time-permits* variants — the primary detector is the AE/LSTM-over-fused-feature-vectors pipeline above, and the experiments below target that pipeline.

### 6.3 Baselines it must beat

A two-tier baseline story the rubric rewards:

- **Tier 1 — deterministic structural and per-modality non-ML.** The structural BIST baselines (#3, #4, N3, N5) frame the honest pitch: *not* "we detect shorts better" but "we detect the degradation modes BIST leaves on the table, online, from telemetry." Per-modality non-ML baselines: per-channel threshold/margin-sweep (#13 eye/BER; #11 WMA-thermal), and a **non-ML multimodal statistical fusion** — PCA / Mahalanobis-distance over the fused feature vector — which no surveyed work builds and is the cleanest "fusion beats single-modality" delta target. If a linear fusion already wins, the deep model's marginal delta becomes the honest claim.
- **Tier 2 — on-chip unsupervised and non-fused ML.** OCTANE (#2) and its predecessor E-SCOUT ported to link telemetry; vanilla AE reconstruction (and #1's both-gradients variant); single-modality versions of our own detector. The cheap-but-strong tabular winners — Random Forest / Boosted Trees / XGBoost (#2, #7, #12) — anchor the lower bound. The proposed multimodal fused detector must beat all on AUROC and *early-warning lead-time* at comparable on-chip PPA.

### 6.4 Data strategy

No public D2D telemetry exists — state this plainly; it is the motivation. The strategy is a **released, physically-grounded, characterized fault-injection generator** that the field's own practice legitimizes (injected anomalies are the norm, §4), with the artifact itself promoted to a *first-class contribution* — schema, ground-truth labels, license, and versioned train/test splits — not a by-product. Components: the **P3405/UCIe-mandated signal set** (eye, BER, skew, retrain, PVT) as the schema [#6]; a **defect→electrical mapping** from Q3D/RC models (#3, N3) to convert physical weak/coupling defects into per-lane timing/eye/BER degradation with graded severity (mirroring #12's void-ratio and #13's ordinal severity); **thermal traces** from a 2.5D/3D-aware interval thermal toolchain (e.g., CoMeT, used by #11); **voltage-noise** from on-chip-noise-sensor characteristics and SSOL/ground-bounce models (N3, N5); and N4's measured temperature-sensitivity constants (1.002 ps/°C, 24.6 ps / 3.70% drift) to calibrate realistic environmental drift.

Three commitments de-risk the synthetic-circularity objection — that a detector might merely invert the simulator that generated its data:

- **Disjoint train/test and held-out severities.** Train on one set of defect classes/severities; test on *unseen* defect classes and severity ranges, exploiting Deep SAD's demonstrated generalization-to-unseen-anomalies property (N2-D3). The fusion-vs-single-modality delta must arise from a *physically-motivated confounder overlap* — concretely, environmental drift calibrated to N4's *measured* 0.645–1.384 ps/°C sensitivities, so the drift is not a trivial parameter the detector can regress away, and single-modality detection *provably fails* for the N4-grounded reason that drift exceeds the anomaly signature — not by generative construction.
- **Validation against measured adjacent-channel data.** At least one result on a *measured* set — Usama's released DRAM-SI data (#1) or Kang's measured Cu interconnects (#13) — to show the synthetic eye/BER degradation distributions match real ones, so not every number is synthetic.
- **Parameter-sensitivity reporting.** Report how results move with the defect→electrical mapping assumptions; if they swing wildly, the benchmark's realism floor is stated honestly rather than hidden.

Anomalies are injected as gradual degradation trajectories (slow-to-rise/fall onset per N3) with a documented ground-truth "time of hard failure" per trajectory, so the early-warning property can be measured (§6.5).

### 6.5 Evaluation plan

Report **AUROC** and precision/recall/F1 (not bare accuracy, given imbalance); **early-warning lead-time**, defined operationally as the number of cycles/epochs between the detector's first sustained flag *at a fixed operating point* (a chosen FPR with a precision floor) and the trajectory's ground-truth hard-failure time — the property that justifies the whole approach, made a reproducible measurement rather than a slogan; **PPA** against the structural baselines (#3/#4 numbers as comparators) if claiming on-die, or an honest "offline analyzer" disclaimer otherwise (see §6.6); and a **noise/drift robustness sweep** following #13's 5/10/15 dB protocol and N4's temperature-stress protocol — cheap differentiation almost no one else does, and which the project commits to actually running. Include a case study visualizing a single lane's telemetry, its fused score over time, and the flagged degradation onset (à la N1's localized scoring).

### 6.6 Deployment story, course-rubric, and DATE-2027 alignment

**Primary deployment framing: offline/near-line analyzer, with an on-die fast-path as a stretch goal.** We pick one to avoid trying to have it both ways. The primary detector is an *offline/near-line analyzer* that ingests buffered telemetry — this removes the obligation to fit an LSTM in the ~1%-area / ~0.5%-average-power envelope that #2/#11/N4 show is feasible only for lightweight detectors. As a stretch goal, an on-die fast-path uses a *quantized/distilled* student of the LSTM (or the multiplier-free statistical template of #11) with a *target* footprint stated against that envelope; if it does not fit, the offline framing is the committed fallback and is stated as such throughout, rather than implied to be on-die.

The project then satisfies every rubric element: a *crisp problem definition* (online D2D link-health, a real blind spot with standard-mandated signals); a *data-source explanation* (released, characterized, physically-grounded fault-injection generator, honest that no public set exists and validated against measured adjacent data); a *method design* with explicit baseline (deterministic BIST + non-ML PCA/Mahalanobis fusion) and AI method (AE/LSTM fusion + Deep SAD) with defined inputs/outputs; *experiments* with detection metrics, defined early-warning lead-time, runtime, robustness sweeps, visualization, and a case study; and *technical insight* (why fusion works — environmental common-mode cancellation reveals the true degradation signal; and AI's limits — synthetic data, black-box explainability, on-die budget). It deliberately avoids over-engineering — the AE/LSTM-AD and statistical detectors are squarely the recommended methods, and no method novelty is claimed — and avoids being broad-but-empty by focusing on one concrete problem. For DATE-2027 Track-T, the defensible framing is: *the first open, characterized, online, multimodal D2D link-health benchmark and detector*, with a non-ML baseline + ML delta, physically-grounded-and-validated released data, and honest limitations.

## 7. Limitations and Open Challenges

We state the candid caveats a Track-T reviewer expects.

**Synthetic/emulated data.** No released silicon D2D telemetry exists, so the benchmark is synthetic-but-physically-grounded; its realism rests on the fidelity of the Q3D/RC defect→electrical mapping (#3, N3) and the thermal/voltage models, not on measured field aging — which is precisely why §6.4 commits to characterizing the generator, validating it against measured adjacent-channel data, and reporting parameter sensitivity rather than asserting realism.

**Fusion validated on standards-derived models, not measured chiplets.** The multimodal correlations are parameterized from the P3405/UCIe signal set and from FPGA/lab-vehicle measurements (N4, #14), so transfer to a productized UCIe PHY is by analogy.

**Borrowed degradation physics.** The degradation trajectories borrow physics from solder-joint/RF-impedance (#12, #13) and BTI/aging studies (N4, N7) rather than measured D2D link aging.

**Importance of the online axis is argued, not measured.** As §1.2 concedes, no surveyed source gives a D2D in-field FIT/RMA rate; the case for runtime monitoring over better manufacturing test rests on documented degradation physics and a standards call, not on a published prevalence figure.

**Black-box explainability.** An AE/LSTM detector flags but does not explain; a deterministic confirmation step (#3/#4/N3) is needed before any repair, and explainability of *which modality* drove a flag is an open problem.

**On-die PPA budget.** If the stretch-goal on-die fast-path is pursued, the temporal model must fit the ~1% area / ~0.5% average power envelope that #2/#11/N4 demonstrate is feasible only for lightweight detectors — an LSTM may need quantization/distillation, and the committed fallback is the offline analyzer of §6.6.

**Multi-vendor / federated angle.** Chiplets come from different fabs (#6, N6), so a detector trained on one vendor's "normal" may not transfer; a federated or per-vendor-calibrated variant, and the trust-separation model of #11/N4 (untrusted PHY IP, trusted detector), are open directions.

**Detection ≠ verified repair.** An ML detector cannot guarantee correctness the way deterministic test (#3, #4, #6) does, so the strongest framing keeps ML in the flag-and-rank role with a deterministic check before action.

**Search scope.** As §1.4 and §5 state, the negative "no work fuses…" claim is scoped to the surveyed, seeded corpus plus targeted searches; in particular the broader PHM/fusion literature was not systematically surveyed, and the defensible claim is the narrow one of §5(1).

## 8. Conclusion

The chiplet shift has turned the die-to-die interconnect into the dominant reliability bottleneck of heterogeneous systems, with tens of thousands of micro-bumps per package and in-field degradation modes that one-time structural test cannot foresee — and that no single telemetry modality can disentangle from benign environmental drift, since measured interposer delay drifts 24.6 ps (3.70%) over a 24.57 °C rise, exceeding many anomaly signatures (N4). This survey of nineteen papers shows a field rich in the *ingredients* of a solution — rigorous D2D structural test and defect physics (#3, #4, #6, N3, N5, N6, N7, N8), lightweight on-chip anomaly engines with hierarchical scoring and graded response (#2, #11), label-scarce ML anomaly methods (#1, N1, N2-D3, #8, #7), and electrical-signature diagnosis with concrete confounder-handling (#12, #13, #14, N4) — but with the key cell empty: *no surveyed work fuses voltage, thermal, and link telemetry for online D2D link health*. Within the corpus, multimodal fusion appears only for hard-fault electrical-signature diagnosis on adjacent channels; D2D-native work is deterministic and hard-fault; on-die detectors are single-modality. The opportunity is precise and well-bounded: an online, multimodal-telemetry-fusion detector (autoencoder/LSTM with hierarchical per-lane → per-link graded scoring and N4-style confounder rejection) that flags gradual D2D link degradation before failure, benchmarked against the structural BIST it complements, on-chip unsupervised detectors, and a non-ML statistical-fusion control, and released as a characterized, physically-grounded fault-injection benchmark validated against measured adjacent-channel data. Framed honestly as a *domain + benchmark* contribution rather than a new ML method, with the importance of the online axis argued from physics rather than from an unavailable field-failure rate, this is a credible, non-over-engineered, course-aligned contribution that fills genuine white space and is defensible as a DATE-2027 Track-T paper.
